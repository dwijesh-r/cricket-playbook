## The Headline

**The Best Top Three in the IPL Meets the Hardest Question: Who Finishes the Innings?**

Gujarat Titans scored 192.8 runs per match in 2025, the highest in franchise history. Shubman Gill (155.7 SR, 1,263 balls, HIGH), Sai Sudharsan (148.7 SR, 1,108 balls, HIGH), and Jos Buttler (149.0 SR, 865 balls, HIGH) form arguably the most complete top three in the tournament. Yet GT were eliminated in the knockouts, because the gap between overs 1-15 and overs 16-20 on the bowling side is a chasm the pace attack has not figured out how to cross. The death bowling economy went from 9.44 in 2023 to 10.94 in 2025. Until someone plugs that leak, the best top order in the IPL will keep watching from the couch in late May.

## The Story

The Gujarat Titans' four-season arc reads like a screenplay with an unresolved third act. Champions in their debut year (2022, 75.0% win rate). Finalists in 2023 (64.7%). A collapse to 41.7% in 2024. Then a muscular resurgence in 2025: nine wins from 15, a batting average of 192.8 per match, and a return to the playoffs. The 2025 campaign confirmed that GT's batting core is among the best in the league. It also confirmed that their bowling, once the franchise's calling card under Hardik Pandya, is now the structural weakness holding them back.

The numbers are unambiguous. In 2023, GT's death bowling economy was an elite 9.44 with a 32.2% dot ball rate. By 2025, that had ballooned to 10.94 with a 27.3% dot rate. The middle overs told a similar story: 8.16 economy in 2023, 9.10 in 2025. The batting masked it. Gill's middle-overs SR of 160.2 (568 balls, HIGH), Sudharsan's 145.8 (550 balls, HIGH), and Buttler's 150.3 (384 balls, MEDIUM) meant GT could post totals that papered over the bowling cracks. But in high-leverage playoff cricket, the cracks became fractures. GT lost their last three matches of 2025, conceding 9.54 in the powerplay and 10.94 at the death when defending.

The off-season response was remarkably restrained. GT retained all 20 players. No auction purchases. No trades. This is a franchise that looked at its squad and decided the answer was continuity, not disruption. The bet is straightforward: Rashid Khan (18.00 Cr, retained) rediscovers his 2023 form (8.21 economy, 27 wickets), Prasidh Krishna (9.50 Cr, retained) builds on his breakout 2025 (25 wickets, 8.34 economy), and the pace trio of Siraj-Rabada-Prasidh learns how to bowl at the death without hemorrhaging runs.

The central question for 2026 is not whether GT can score runs. They can. It is whether the bowling attack can hold what the batting sets. A 192.8 average score means nothing if the opposition regularly chases it down. And with Rashid Khan's economy rising from 8.21 in 2023 to 9.30 in 2025, even the franchise's most reliable weapon is no longer the certainty it once was.

If the bowling reverts to 2023 levels, GT are genuine title contenders. If the 2025 decline is structural rather than cyclical, the best batting lineup in the IPL will once again exit before the final.

## Team Style Analysis

GT's tactical identity has swung from bowling-first efficiency to batting-powered aggression in three seasons. The data captures a franchise in evolution.

**2023: The Shami-Rashid Machine**

| Phase | Batting SR | Bowling Economy |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Powerplay | 137.1 | 8.61 |
| Middle | 137.2 | 8.16 |
| Death | 183.2 | 9.44 |

GT won the 2023 title race as finalists (lost the final to CSK) on the back of Mohammed Shami's 28 wickets and Rashid Khan's 27. The bowling was elite across all three phases: 8.16 middle-overs economy was the best in the league, and 9.44 at the death was second-best. The batting was secondary, top-heavy (65.9% from the top 3), and efficient rather than explosive.

**2024: The Valley**

| Phase | Batting SR | Bowling Economy | Delta from 2023 |
|-------|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| Powerplay | 121.5 | 9.53 | Bat: -15.6 / Bowl: +0.92 |
| Middle | 133.3 | 8.87 | Bat: -3.9 / Bowl: +0.71 |
| Death | 167.4 | 11.43 | Bat: -15.8 / Bowl: +1.99 |

Everything collapsed. Powerplay batting SR dropped 15.6 points. Death bowling economy spiked by nearly 2 runs per over. Without Shami (injured), the bowling disintegrated. Only Rashid Khan's 8.52 middle-overs economy held any structural integrity.

**2025: The Batting Explosion**

| Phase | Batting SR | Bowling Economy | Delta from 2024 |
|-------|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| Powerplay | 148.5 | 9.83 | Bat: +27.0 / Bowl: +0.30 |
| Middle | 160.4 | 9.10 | Bat: +27.1 / Bowl: +0.23 |
| Death | 169.6 | 10.94 | Bat: +2.2 / Bowl: -0.49 |

GT solved the batting problem emphatically. Powerplay SR surged 27 points. Middle-overs SR of 160.4 was among the league's best. But the bowling continued to deteriorate: powerplay economy hit 9.83 (the worst of the three seasons), and the middle overs leaked 9.10. The death bowling improved marginally from the 2024 disaster but remained well above the 2023 standard. GT became a team that outscores opponents rather than outbowling them. That works in the league phase. It does not work in knockouts.

**Squad-Building Philosophy:** GT have committed to continuity over disruption. The 20-player squad is built around an elite batting top three, a world-class leg-spinner, and a pace attack with high ceilings and uneven floors. Eight all-rounders provide flexibility, but the lack of a proven death-bowling specialist beyond Prasidh Krishna (10.39 death economy, 108 balls, HIGH) is the squad's defining structural gap. The philosophy is clear: trust the core, back the trajectory, and bet that a full pre-season together will fix what a transfer window could not.
