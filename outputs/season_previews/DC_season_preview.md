## The Headline

**The Spin Twins Have Arrived. Now Delhi Needs Everything Else to Follow.**

Kuldeep Yadav (left-arm wrist-spin; 7.82 economy, 41 wickets, 145 overs since 2023, HIGH) and Axar Patel (left-arm orthodox; 7.86 economy, 27 wickets, 127 overs, HIGH) give Delhi Capitals the most potent left-arm spin pairing in IPL 2026. They rank 8th and 21st respectively in the bowler composite rankings. But spin alone didn't save the franchise from an 18-25 record across the last three seasons, and the batting profile reveals a squad that strikes at 135.4 against fast bowling (619 balls, HIGH), a full 17.2 points below the league average. The question for 2026 is whether KL Rahul's captaincy and a reshaped squad can convert elite spin into a playoff campaign, or whether the same structural weaknesses that have plagued Delhi since 2020 will persist behind a different scoreboard graphic.

## The Story

The last three seasons have been an exercise in competitive mediocrity for Delhi Capitals. Five wins in 2023. Seven in 2024. Six in 2025. An 18-25 record that translates to a 41.9% win rate, placing Delhi firmly in the lower half of the table across the entire window. The franchise has not reached a playoff since 2021. For a team that reached the final in 2020, the regression has been both steady and relentless.

The numbers chart the decline in phases. Powerplay batting SR climbed from 130.8 in 2023 to 168.1 in 2024, before falling back to 138.5 in 2025. Middle-overs batting went from 119.8 to 141.7 over the same window, the one genuine improvement. But the bowling has been a persistent problem: powerplay economy of 8.05 in 2023 ballooned to 10.65 in 2024, settling at 9.86 in 2025. Death bowling economy of 11.15 in 2023 peaked at 12.44 in 2024, moderating to 10.56 in 2025 but still above the league average of 10.87. The franchise has been leaking runs at both ends while the batting hasn't been consistent enough to compensate.

The off-season response centred on stability over revolution. KL Rahul (14.00 Cr, retained) arrived in 2025 and takes captaincy for his second season, bringing the most complete top-order batting profile in the squad: 135.9 SR across 981 balls (HIGH) with a 43.0 average. Axar Patel (16.50 Cr, retained) remains the most expensive player and the franchise's heartbeat, the only player in the squad who contributes meaningfully with both bat and ball. Kuldeep Yadav (13.25 Cr, retained) anchors the bowling. Mitchell Starc (11.75 Cr, retained) provides the left-arm pace X-factor, if the franchise can find the version of Starc that takes wickets without haemorrhaging runs.

The central question for 2026 is straightforward: can Delhi convert elite spin resources into team results? The spin pairing of Kuldeep and Axar is objectively among the best in the league. But the pace bowling has been expensive (Starc at 10.67 economy, Mukesh Kumar at 10.55, T Natarajan at 9.33), the batting below the league average against fast bowling, and the death overs a persistent vulnerability on both sides of the ball. If the spin twins can carry the middle overs and the batting depth from Stubbs, Ashutosh Sharma, and the new acquisitions can compensate at the death, Delhi have a genuine playoff argument. If the pace bowling remains a liability and the batting continues to underperform against quality fast bowling, this franchise is headed for a fifth consecutive season outside the top four.

## Off-Season Changes

**Captaincy:** KL Rahul (14.00 Cr, retained) continues as captain after arriving in 2025. His L10 SR of 141.0 (251 balls) is 4.6 points above his career mark of 135.9, suggesting a player in stable form entering his first full season leading this franchise. The challenge: transforming Delhi's 41.9% win rate into something that resembles a playoff contender.

**Key Departures (from 2025 squad):** The most significant departures from the 2024-2025 window include Rishabh Pant (to CSK/LSG), who was the franchise's identity player for half a decade. While Pant left before 2025, the gap he created at the top of the order remains unfilled. From the 2025 squad itself, the franchise has maintained continuity through heavy retention, with 14 players retained at a combined cost of approximately 80 Cr.

**Key Retentions:** DC retained 14 players: Axar Patel (16.50 Cr), KL Rahul (14.00 Cr), Kuldeep Yadav (13.25 Cr), Mitchell Starc (11.75 Cr), T Natarajan (10.75 Cr), Tristan Stubbs (10.00 Cr), Mukesh Kumar (8.00 Cr), Abhishek Porel (4.00 Cr), Ashutosh Sharma (3.80 Cr), Sameer Rizvi (0.95 Cr), Dushmantha Chameera (0.75 Cr), Karun Nair (0.50 Cr), Vipraj Nigam (0.50 Cr), and Madhav Tiwari (0.40 Cr), along with three more at base price. The retention strategy reveals a franchise that believes in its core but needed specific reinforcements.

**Auction Strategy:** DC spent approximately 10.45 Cr on 8 new acquisitions at auction, a relatively modest spend. The headline buy was Auqib Nabi (8.40 Cr), an uncapped right-arm fast bowling all-rounder who represents a significant gamble on pace potential. Pathum Nissanka (3.20 Cr) adds overseas batting depth. David Miller (2.00 Cr) brings proven finishing pedigree. Lungi Ngidi (1.00 Cr), Kyle Jamieson (0.75 Cr), Ben Duckett (0.75 Cr), Nitish Rana (0.50 Cr), and Prithvi Shaw (0.30 Cr) fill specific gaps at value prices.

**What's Structurally Different from 2025:** Three shifts define the 2026 squad. First, the death-overs batting gains David Miller (154.3 death SR, 219 balls, MEDIUM) and Nitish Rana (191.9 death SR, 37 balls, LOW) as finishing options alongside the established Stubbs and Ashutosh Sharma. Second, the pace bowling adds Auqib Nabi as a potential new-ball partner for Starc or Natarajan, reducing reliance on Mukesh Kumar's expensive 10.55 economy. Third, the overseas batting pool deepens with Miller, Nissanka, Duckett, and Jamieson competing for slots alongside Stubbs, Starc, and Ngidi. The net effect: more options, more competition, and a franchise that is betting on depth rather than star power.

The most analytically verifiable of DC's auction buys. Miller strikes at 154.3 at the death across 219 balls (MEDIUM) since 2023, with an overall career SR of 139.1 across 436 balls (MEDIUM). At 36 and 2.00 Cr, Miller is a low-cost finishing option who fills the death-overs gap. If he regresses further from his L10 SR of 128.6 (119 balls), the bench awaits.

### Lungi Ngidi (1.00 Cr, Auction)

Just 2 IPL matches and 8 overs since 2023 (LOW), but classified as a "Workhorse Seamer" with PP_ELITE and PRESSURE_BUILDER tags from cross-tournament data. The concern: fitness history and IPL adaptation.

### Remaining Depth Buys

Kyle Jamieson (0.75 Cr) offers overseas pace all-rounder depth with 5 wickets at 9.80 economy in 15 overs (LOW). Ben Duckett (0.75 Cr) is a left-handed wicketkeeper-batter providing batting flexibility. Nitish Rana (0.50 Cr) brings 672 runs at 146.1 SR across 460 balls (MEDIUM) since 2023 as a left-handed middle-order option. Prithvi Shaw (0.30 Cr) has 304 runs at 148.3 SR (205 balls, MEDIUM), a former DC player returning at base price. Sahil Parakh (0.30 Cr) is an uncapped 18-year-old left-hander.

**Auction Grade: C+.** The core was already assembled through retention. The auction addressed specific gaps (finishing with Miller, pace depth with Ngidi/Auqib Nabi) at reasonable prices. The 8.40 Cr on Auqib Nabi is the swing factor. If he delivers 15+ wickets, the grade revises to B+. If he doesn't, DC overpaid for insurance.

## Team Style Analysis

Delhi's tactical identity has been a work in progress. The three-season trajectory reveals a franchise that overcorrected in 2024 and is still searching for equilibrium.

### 2023: The Year Everything Stalled

| Phase | Batting SR | Bowling Economy |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Powerplay | 130.8 | 8.05 |
| Middle | 119.8 | 7.84 |
| Death | 128.9 | 11.15 |

The 2023 numbers are startling. A batting SR of 119.8 in the middle overs was the lowest in the league. A death batting SR of 128.9 suggested a team that couldn't accelerate. Only the powerplay bowling (8.05 economy) and middle-overs bowling (7.84) provided genuine quality. This was a team that could contain but couldn't score.

### 2024: The Overcorrection

| Phase | Batting SR | Bowling Economy | Delta from 2023 |
|-------|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| Powerplay | 168.1 | 10.65 | Bat: +37.3 / Bowl: +2.60 |
| Middle | 139.6 | 8.56 | Bat: +19.8 / Bowl: +0.72 |
| Death | 173.1 | 12.44 | Bat: +44.2 / Bowl: +1.29 |

The 2024 batting transformation was dramatic: powerplay SR surged 37.3 points, death SR jumped 44.2 points. But the bowling collapsed. Powerplay economy spiked from 8.05 to 10.65, death economy from 11.15 to 12.44. Delhi won 7 matches, two more than 2023, but the improvement was unsustainable. They were outscoring opponents while simultaneously being outbowled.

### 2025: Searching for Balance

| Phase | Batting SR | Bowling Economy | Delta from 2024 |
|-------|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| Powerplay | 138.5 | 9.86 | Bat: -29.6 / Bowl: -0.79 |

| Death | 176.7 | 10.56 | Bat: +3.6 / Bowl: -1.88 |

The 2025 numbers tell the story of partial progress. Powerplay batting regressed sharply (-29.6 SR), but middle-overs batting held (+2.1) and death batting improved (+3.6). Bowling recovered at the death (-1.88 economy) but remained expensive in the powerplay (9.86). The only consistent positive: middle-overs batting improved every year, from 119.8 to 139.6 to 141.7.

**Squad-Building Philosophy:** Delhi have built a spin-first team wrapped around pace gambles. The retention of both Kuldeep (13.25 Cr) and Axar (16.50 Cr) signals a franchise committed to middle-overs dominance through left-arm spin. The pace attack is a collection of high-ceiling, high-variance options: Starc (elite on his day, expensive on most), Natarajan (workmanlike), Mukesh Kumar (volume wicket-taker at a cost), and Ngidi (upside bet). The batting is anchored by Rahul's accumulation and distributed firepower from Stubbs, Ashutosh, and Axar. It is a squad designed to win low-scoring games through spin and lose high-scoring ones through pace deficiency.
