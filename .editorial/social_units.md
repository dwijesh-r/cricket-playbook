### MI

Sherfane Rutherford was a smarter buy than Allah Ghazanfar or Will Jacks.

Everyone roasted MI for missing out. But Rutherford fills the one gap they actually had: a left-handed death hitter who doesn't block an overseas bowling slot. Ghazanfar gives you mystery spin MI don't need (Santner + Markande). Jacks gives you a top-order bat MI don't need (Rohit + Tilak + SKY).

Rutherford gives you what nobody else in the squad can. That's not a consolation prize. That's roster construction.

### CSK

Noor Ahmad is more valuable to CSK than Ruturaj Gaikwad. Here's the math.

Remove Gaikwad from CSK's XI. Samson and Mhatre still open, Dube and Dhoni still anchor. The machine adjusts.

Remove Noor Ahmad. CSK's middle-overs spin attack disappears. Chepauk needs spin control in overs 7-15 more than it needs run-scoring. CSK's home average since 2023: 171. They don't outscore you. They suffocate you. That plan dies without Noor Ahmad.

### RCB

Bhuvneshwar Kumar, not Virat Kohli, is the most important player in RCB's title defense.

Kohli scored 2,037 runs since 2023 at 56.58 average. Nobody is questioning his output. But RCB won the 2025 title because they became boring. Bhuvi's middle-overs economy is what turned RCB from entertainers into champions.

Remove Kohli and RCB still have Salt, Patidar, Iyer. Remove Bhuvi and the entire bowling system that won the championship collapses.

### KKR

KKR should drop Sunil Narine from the opening slot.

Narine's powerplay SR is declining. But his middle-overs bowling is still elite: 7.5 economy across 148 overs since 2023. That's second only to Bumrah in the entire IPL.

Bat him at 8 where he can still hit 15-ball cameos, keep him fresh for his 4 overs of middle-overs control. Allen and Rahane can open. You don't sacrifice your best bowler's effectiveness to squeeze 20 extra runs from a slot someone else can fill.

### DC

The Miller-Stubbs combination at 5 and 6 is the most dangerous middle-order pairing in the IPL. And DC are wasting them.

Miller is one of the most experienced finishers in T20 history. Stubbs has been elite in the 15-to-20 phase internationally. Together at 5-6, they could dominate overs 10-16, where matches are decided.

Instead, DC have them buried behind Rana and surrounded by left-handers, making it easy for opposition captains to matchup-bowl. Promote Stubbs to 4. Let Miller bat 5 with license. Stop being cautious.

### PBKS

Yuzvendra Chahal is more irreplaceable than Arshdeep Singh for Punjab. This shouldn't be controversial but it is.

Remove Chahal and PBKS have zero frontline spinners in their predicted XI. Zero. In a tournament where middle-overs spin takes 43% of all wickets, Punjab would go in with no first-choice spinner. That's not a risk. That's a structural flaw.

If Jadeja averages 30+ at 5, RR's middle order works. If he gets out cheaply, they're 4 down in the 12th over with no safety net.

### SRH

SRH have the best top 5 in the IPL and the weakest bowling attack. No amount of batting firepower fixes this.

Head, Abhishek, Ishan Kishan, Klaasen, Livingstone. Five players who can individually chase 200. The problem: SRH's death bowling concedes more runs than almost any team in the dataset.

You can build the most destructive batting order in IPL history. But if your bowlers can't hold a 15-run advantage in the last 4 overs, you're relying on winning every toss and batting first. That's not strategy. That's hope.

### GT

Washington Sundar should bat at 4 for Gujarat Titans.

Left-handed. Clean striker through the off side. Can rotate strike without letting the rate drop below 8. His T20I batting over the last two years shows genuine growth, not just slogging but innings-building.

At 5, he comes in too late to anchor, too early to just hit. At 4, he gets enough balls to play his natural game, and his left-handedness disrupts right-arm off-spin at the spin-friendly Narendra Modi Stadium. GT trust him to bowl 4 overs. Trust him to bat 30 balls.

### LSG

Rishabh Pant's second season at LSG will define the franchise.

Year one was adjustment. New dressing room, new teammates, new franchise culture. Year two is when 27 Cr has to deliver results.

LSG built the entire squad around him: Pooran and Markram as overseas support, Badoni as the understudy, Mayank Yadav as the pace spearhead. If Pant delivers a full, fit, commanding season, LSG are genuine playoff contenders. If not, they have a 27 Cr anchor and no Plan B.
