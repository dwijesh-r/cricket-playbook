**Issue:** The all-time records are useful context, but the section still lacks 2026-specific implications for most matchups. The Samson note at the bottom (line 297) hints at this, but every row in the table should have a 2026 implication.

**Example Fix:**
- **MI (46.2% win rate):** "CSK's worst record against a major rival. The loss of Pathirana (who took 12 wickets at Wankhede across 2023-24) makes this matchup even harder."

**Impact if Fixed:** This would push the section from "useful reference" to "tactical intelligence briefing." Without it, the section remains the weakest in the piece, but it is no longer blocking.

### 2. Full Squad Table (Bench) Remains Dense

**Issue:** Rows 13-25 in the Full Squad Table are still pure reference material with minimal narrative value. Players like Ramakrishna Ghosh, Mukesh Choudhary, and Shreyas Gopal get a row but no story.

**Current State:** This is a stat pack appendix embedded in a magazine feature. It works as a reference but adds little to the reading experience.

**Suggested Fix (Non-Blocking):** Move the full bench to a collapsible section or appendix link. Keep only the top 3-4 bench players (Rahul Chahar, Matt Henry, Matthew Short) in the main table with one-sentence selection scenarios for each.

**Impact if Fixed:** This would improve pacing and reduce the visual density of the piece. But as written, it is functional.

---

### 3. Innings Context Insight Underexploited

**Issue:** The "CSK should bat first" insight (line 219) is buried in a table. This is a fantasy-league-relevant, tactically significant finding that deserves a bolder callout.

**Suggested Fix (Non-Blocking):** Add a pull quote or bolded summary: "If CSK win the toss, the data says bat. Every time. Their setting SR of 150.0 is 10.1 points higher than their chasing SR of 139.9."

**Impact if Fixed:** This would make the insight more discoverable for casual readers and increase shareability among fantasy cricket audiences.

### 4. Verdict Section Could End Stronger

**Issue:** The closing line ("how it ended, not how long it lasted") is good but not great. The last sentence is what people remember. It needs to hit harder.

**Suggested Fix (Non-Blocking):** Tie back to the Dhoni opener with a closing image: "Sometimes dynasties don't end with a press conference. They end with a dot ball. CSK's 2026 season is the space between the last great moment and the final bow."

**Impact if Fixed:** This would create narrative symmetry and leave the reader with a stronger emotional impression. But as written, the Verdict section is functional.
